Exercise 1.2

Recurrent Relation:

\(A(0)=0 \\ A(n)=3A(n-1)+2, \text{for }n\ge0\)

Closed Form Hypothesis:

\(A(n)=3^n-1\)

Inductive Proof:

\(A(0)=3^0-1=0 \\ A(n)=3(3^{n-1}-1)+2=3^n-1\)

Exercise 1.11a

Recurrent Relation:

\(D(0)=0 \\ D(2n)=2D(2n-2)+2, \text{for }n\ge0\)

Closed Form Hypothesis:

\(D(2n)=2^{n+1}-2\)

Inductive Proof:

\(D(2(0))=2^{0+1}-2=0 \\ s = 2n-2 \\ D(s) = 2^{(s/2)+1}-2 \\ D(2n)=2D(s)+2=2^{(s/2)+2}-4+2=2^{n+1}-2\)

This is only a partial solution, because it doesn't solve for \(I(2)\)

Recurrent Relation:

\(I(1)=0 \\ I(2)=2 \\ I(2n)=2I(n)-1,\text{ for }n>0 \\ I(2n+1)=2I(n)+1,\text{ for }n>0\)

Closed Form Hypothesis:

\(n=3(2^m)+l,\text{ where }0\le l<3(2^m)\le n \\ I(n)=2l+1,\text{ for }n>2\)

Inductive Proof:

Basis:\(I(3)=I(3(2^0)+0)=2(0)+1=1\)
Even:\(I(n)=2(2(l/2)+1)-1=2l+1,\text{ for }n>2\)
Odd:\(I(n)=2(2(l-1)/2+1)+1=2l+1,\text{ for }n>2\)
